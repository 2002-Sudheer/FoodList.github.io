Chicken Sukka

About Chicken Sukka

Chicken Sukka is a traditional Indian dry chicken dish originating from the coastal regions of Karnataka. This dish is known for its rich and aromatic blend of spices, coconut, and tender chicken pieces. Chicken Sukka is a dry dish that pairs wonderfully with rice, roti, or neer dosa.

Cooking Type

Chicken Sukka is cooked using a slow dry-roasting method where the chicken is simmered with spices and grated coconut, giving it a unique and flavorful taste. It involves saut√©ing the chicken with onions, spices, and freshly grated coconut until the moisture evaporates, leaving behind a semi-dry dish.

History

Chicken Sukka has its roots in the coastal regions of Karnataka and Maharashtra, particularly popular in the Mangalorean cuisine. The dish reflects the local influence of coconut-based cooking and a blend of regional spices, making it a favorite among the people of coastal Karnataka and beyond.
