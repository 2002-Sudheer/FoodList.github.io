Poori Baji

About Poori Baji

Poori Baji is a popular Indian breakfast dish consisting of puffed, deep-fried bread known as poori, served with a flavorful potato curry called baji. The dish is known for its crispy and golden-brown poori, paired with the spicy and tangy flavors of the potato curry.

Cooking Type

Poori is deep-fried in hot oil, while the baji is a stir-fried dish with boiled potatoes, onions, spices, and curry leaves. The combination of the crispy poori and soft, spiced potatoes makes it a perfect breakfast or snack.

History of Poori Baji

The origins of poori can be traced back to ancient India, where it was considered a delicacy in royal feasts. Over time, it became a staple in Indian households, especially in North and South India, where it is enjoyed as a breakfast dish. Poori Baji is also served during festivals and special occasions across various regions in India.
