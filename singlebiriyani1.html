Biryani

About Non-Veg Biryani

Non-Veg Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish made with marinated meat, basmati rice, and a blend of rich spices. Often prepared with chicken, lamb, or beef, it is a beloved dish across South Asia and the Middle East, celebrated for its unique mix of flavors and textures.

Cooking Method

Non-Veg Biryani is traditionally cooked using the Dum Pukht method, which involves slow-cooking the meat and rice in layers under a tightly sealed lid, allowing the ingredients to cook in their own steam, intensifying the flavors.

History of Biryani

The origins of Biryani trace back to the Mughal era, where it was a royal dish prepared in the kitchens of the emperors. It is believed to have been brought to India by Persian travelers and traders, evolving over time to become one of the most popular dishes in South Asian cuisine.
